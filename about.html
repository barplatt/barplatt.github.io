About

Always looking for the perfect stack, that’s me. The perfect stack lets me get back to focusing more on the design and flow of things and less worrying about where things may break. For this site I tried out Middleman and I am very pleased with how little setup it required. Leaving much of the nuts and bolts of the CSS to the experts I chose to integrate Bourbon into the flow (but I prefer Irish if we are talking about the beverage). For those not familiar with this framework it uses primarily a bunch of mixins so that you really only pull in what you need from it. There are pros and cons to everything of course but for something quite small in scope as this site you end up with less unused CSS. This is quite the opposite approach to something like Bootstrap where you mostly over-ride the CSS and unless you are willing to hack the framework itself you’ll end up with plenty of unused classes wasting precious bandwidth.

Enough tech talk, what do I know anyway, I am from the fluffy liberal arts world and the technical bits have been scooped up along the way…quite a long way. Netscape was still the king of the hill when I designed and coded up my first web site. Alta vista was the search engine of choice and search results were mostly useless. Every search term seemed to lead to the same type of page showing some naked parts of the female anatomy, but I digress.

For anyone who has read this far I should mention that my interest and expertise covers everything from web design, graphic design, photography, video shooting and editing. Perhaps this comes with more experience or the types of project I have recently been involved in but I seem to excel in applying that extra layer of polish to a design, and I get results. I can recognize potential risks and complications months in advance. In the gamble of design, I usually bet on the right color. People trust my judgment.

In addition I have participated in the entire software development life cycle from requirements gathering, mockups, and prototypes to developing front-end UIs and testing. In connection to this, my writing skills are also put to the test from time to time writing technical documents and training material. But what I can say in the end is that I consider myself lucky—I actually have fun at work, doing what I enjoy.
